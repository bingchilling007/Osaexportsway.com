Temperature Integrity

Our Cold Chain Logistics System

Every degree matters. Our end-to-end refrigerated supply chain ensures that the quality achieved during processing reaches you perfectly preserved.

🏭
Processing & Blast Freezing
Step 01
Immediately post-slaughter and inspection, meat enters blast freezers operating at -40°C. This rapid temperature reduction locks in freshness, halts microbial activity, and preserves cellular structure — critical for export quality.
Blast freeze to -18°C within 2 hours of slaughter
Continuous temperature monitoring via digital loggers
Segregated cold rooms for different product categories
📦
Vacuum Packaging & Labelling
Step 02
Each cut is individually wrapped or vacuum-sealed in food-grade multilayer packaging within the controlled cold environment. Labels include product name, weight, cut specification, slaughter date, best-before date, and certification marks.
Automated vacuum-sealing at <100 mbar
Bi-lingual labelling (English & Arabic) available
Custom branding and private-label options
🚛
Refrigerated Land Transport
Step 03
Packed cartons are loaded onto our fleet of HACCP-certified refrigerated trucks for transport to the port facility. Trucks maintain -18°C throughout transit, with GPS tracking and automated temperature alerts for complete visibility.
GPS-enabled reefer fleet with live temperature feeds
Pre-cooled vehicle compartments before loading
Sealed and locked cargo holds with tamper evidence
🚢
Reefer Container Shipping
Step 04
Cargo is loaded into pre-inspected 20-ft or 40-ft refrigerated (reefer) containers. We work with leading shipping lines offering regular sailing schedules to Dubai (Jebel Ali), Dammam, Doha, Muscat, and Kuwait City.
Remote container temperature monitoring throughout voyage
Phytosanitary and customs clearance handled end-to-end
Estimated transit: 5–14 days depending on destination

Packaging Standards

Our packaging has been designed to meet — and exceed — the import requirements of all five GCC markets. We use food-grade, BPA-free multilayer vacuum bags and HACCP-approved carton boxes, with every package complying with GCC Standard SASO/GSO 9.
